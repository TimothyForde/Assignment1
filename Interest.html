Interest in IT

What got me into this course and IT in general

• What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start? Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).
My interest in IT is very broad and i'm looking into areas ranging from security systems to how USB game controllers work.
It started when my dad got an intel 486 personal computer for the household back when it first come out, which lead to everyone playing video games on it. I’m told I never really learned to read well untill I suddenly found it a requirement to play those games, and the game manuals back then were rather large and complex. From that time onwards I’ve always had a computer in the house, even if only for gaming.
These days I build and maintain my own PCs. In fact, right now I have 2 desktops I use for my video game streaming. One to play the game and one dedicated as a video encoder/editor. So far, my IT experience is hobby based with only very basic use at work.

• Why did you choose to come to RMIT?
To be honest I first made plans to study with Open University Australia. As someone in the security industry I work 24 7 rotating shifts and from the past I knew that traditional study would not be possible. The ability to be able to sleep after a night shift and only view a lecture or tutorial after a good sleep is very important to me. I went with IT as my interest in the area has only been growing in the last few years with streaming. I also know many people already working in IT and I’m even dating a programmer.
As for RMIT itself? I’ve studied my engineering with them in the past and my only issues were how inflexible the learning was to someone working. I was very happy to find out RMIT offered just the course I wanted online and living only 5km or so north of the city campus I can even drop in if ever required.

• What do you expect to learn during your studies?
I want to take my understanding of computers from my current hobby level to a professional level. I want to learn all the basics of programming so that I can build on it after the course and work on several projects that are currently on my to-do list. I want to learn more about networking, databases, and all the little things that go on in the background at a corporate environment.
I want to gain a greater in-depth understanding of how computers work so I can do things like program and build Human Input Devices via USB. I want to learn some of the basics of how hacking and IT security works in case I want or need to learn more later. And last of course I want to learn enough about IT to use it in a career.
